What even is a "Virtual Card?"

Sezzle's in-store BNPL feature

Launching in Q1 2026, the virtual card lets users generate a temporary Visa card inside the app and tap to pay at partnered physical locations using Sezzle's buy now, pay later Visa card.

What it is

A temporary Visa card generated in the app, tied to a specific merchant, used in-store via tap to pay Sezzle's BNPL credit at a physical register

Where it works

Only at the merchant you select 500+ in-store locations across Canada

In-store Virtual Card In progress

Map-based store locator + in-app education for Virtual Card

Timeline Q1–Q2 2026
Acceptance criteria

Fix comprehension add education screens into the VC tab and card creation flow

Fix discoverability replace the flat merchant list with a map-based store locator

Solution, part 1

Answering "wait, what is this?" before they have to ask

Users didn't understand what the virtual card was or how it differed from paying online. The fix wasn't a tooltip or a help article it was building the explanation directly into the flow at the moments they needed it.

Introducing VC + FAQ

The VC tab now surfaces an explanation screen for first-time users and an FAQ of common questions, so users can get their questions answered immediately without having to go looking.

Why VC vs gift cards?

A lot of the confusion in support tickets was specifically around how gift cards and virtual cards differ and since both can show up in the same VC tab, it wasn't obvious. These screens explain the distinction directly in the flow.

Solution, part 2

Three iterations to get the map right

Problem 2 was discoverability users couldn't tell if there was a store near them. The solution was adding a map banner/button at the top of the store list where users can view stores by location near them. Getting there took three rounds of iteration.

Reflection

What I took away from this one

🗓️

Know what to ship now and what to defer

Not every good idea makes the cut right away. Store open/closed hours was something we wanted but without that data available yet, it was a deliberate call to scope it out and revisit it later rather than hold up the release.

📊

Data turns opinion into direction

Having ticket volume, session replays, and survey numbers meant every design decision had something concrete behind it much easier to align on than instinct alone.
