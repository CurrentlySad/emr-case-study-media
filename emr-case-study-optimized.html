Some background + how I got here

What even is an EMR tablet?

Electronic medical records (EMR) are digital versions of paper patient charts, documenting a patient's medical history, medications, allergies, and vitals. In Lumeto's VR simulations, learners access them through an in-world tablet.

What it is

Digital patient chart used in every VR training session

The issue

Confusing to use, hard to understand, felt visually out of place

Why it matters

Used in almost all training scenarios. Getting it wrong has real learning impact

I asked more about it and found out: the tablet was created by a dev... Not bashing devs, but I thought this could use some work.

Issue 01

Information overload, horizontal space not used efficiently

Everything is crammed into view with no grouping or visual breathing room.

Issue 02

Inconsistent typography

Too many text sizes and weights with no clear system. Users can't tell what's most important at a glance.

Issue 03

No hover or pressed states

Users don't know what page they're on or whether their input has registered at all.

VR Challenges

Designing in VR for the first time

Despite knowing the issues, I faced other challenges while designing in VR for the first time. My biggest hurdle was:

  • Unlike 2D, there's no standard button size in VR. The further away a user stands, the smaller everything feels
  • The size of the tablet itself in VR space also matters — too small and it's hard to read, too large and it blocks the simulation
  • No amount of Figma previewing could replace putting the headset on and testing for real

Side Nav Bar

Starting with what's on every screen

First I started with the side navigation bar since this would be consistent through every screen.

Added the device name

Helps orient learners who are new to Lumeto and this training simulation.

Added hover and pressed states

Users need to know what page they're on and that their input registered, especially in a fast-paced training scenario.

Colours consistent with the web portal design system

The VR tablet now feels like the same product family as the rest of Lumeto's platform.

Icons and labels for each tab

Pairing icons with text labels makes tabs faster to recognise, especially for learners focused on the simulation.

Call a Code moved to the bottom

Separated from the main navigation so it can't be triggered by accident. Emergency actions are styled in red and placed apart so they require deliberate intent.

Reflection

What I learned

🥽

3D design is hard!

Learning a completely new design environment was challenging but really rewarding. VR has its own rules and constraints that you just can't fully grasp until you're in the headset.

📐

2D in Figma doesn't always translate to 3D

What looks great on a flat screen can feel totally different floating in space. Spacing, sizing, and hierarchy all needed rethinking for the medium.

Call a Code

Moved to a pop-up to prevent accidental triggers — a deliberate UX decision to separate emergency actions from everyday navigation.
